INVESTIGATION

To begin with my investigation, I started researching into diverse ways people have used technology and embedded systems to enhance various aspects of wellbeing. I knew that the goal of the project was to improve someone's life and enhance their wellbeing. From my research I found lots of ways technology and embedded systems are used to enhance people's wellbeing (i.e. home appliances, Smartphones, Medical use etc.). Although sometimes the instant impact of these devices would not be massive, over time they helped tremendously with someone's wellbeing.

After looking at many different sources and systems I decided that a project surrounding stress levels would be perfect for me. Throughout the leaving cert stress was a big problem in my life and I wanted to create a way to analyse my stress. As I was working on this project leading up to the mock exams, I noticed how big of a problem stress was and how a way to analyse my stress would be perfect for my computer science project. To ensure my project would be useful to people in a similar situation to me I carried out a survey on 60 students in my school with a handful from another school. From this study I found that 78% of students were stressed coming up to the mock exams. From conducting my research, I now knew that my idea would be beneficial to people in 6th year.

 

Survey Of Students Asked If They Were Feeling Stressed In School:

From my research into stress in students, I found an article by the Irish Independent. This article stated that "Once toxic stress hits, we can find ourselves unable to recognise the signs. Perhaps we begin to snap at our friends and family more than usual. Perhaps we begin to cancel plans or give up activities that bring us joy, such as sports or hobbies. Perhaps we avoid our school-related workload and lean all too willingly into our social life, part-time jobs, or other interests." This helped me understand further the importance of understanding patterns of stress and how to cope with it.

I knew I would need to do some research into reducing stress levels quickly. During my research I came across meditation and from further research decided it was a perfect way for me to help students reduce stress in school.

PLAN AND DESIGN

I started thinking of ways in which I could tackle this problem through use of computer science. I used computational thinking to produce the plan for my idea. I decided that having a quick way for the user to reduce stress through meditation would be essential. I concluded that I would use a BBC Microbit for multiple reasons. First, the Microbit fitted all the requirements I needed for my embedded system. It had a display screen, sound, two buttons and had the ability to communicate through the serial port. I was also very acquainted with the Microbit from using it throughout the leaving cert course.

I had three inputs needed for my data analysis: the time of day, the users stress level before and after their meditation session. When starting the device, it will prompt the user to input the time of day it was right then (input would be a 24hr clock and go up in 1 hour increments i.e. 1:00,2:00 etc). For stress level, the user will input their current stress level at that time, the device will then go into the meditation stage, in which the device would start a timer for 30 seconds for the user to meditate. Once the time is up the device will alert the user through sound and a flashing screen that the meditation is over. The user will then be asked to input their new stress level. These will be the inputs from the user for the theme of wellbeing. The Microbit will prompt the user to input this data when the Microbit is powered on for the Microbit to be fully automated.

The outputs of the system would be the LED screen, sound when the meditation was done and the data analysis in Python all supporting the theme of wellbeing. I will use Python to validate and store the data into a CSV file so that the data can be analysed. Using the Python module Matplotlib I will graph the data in a way that the user can understand and use it to predict further actions they can take to improve their stress levels.

Using Python and the Scikit-learn module I will then create a computer model based on my personally created dataset. For my data I was tracking the workload, exercise hours, sleep quality and stress level for that day. I will then propose 2 "what if questions."" These questions will include my 3 parameters using 2 different data types (Integers and Floats) and it will offer the user insights into what they could change to improve their predicted stress. The user will be able to view the data graphically using Matplotlib and be able to view the results of a "what if" scenario.

EVALUATION

In conclusion, this is my completed project for the brief. This is my interpretation of the brief and I understand that there are different approaches to solving this problem. I think this project is a great way for those struggling with stress in school to deal with their stress. It gives the user a way to destress themselves (Using Meditation) and a way to analyse the results of the meditation (Graph of Pre and Post stress). The user also has a way to ask what if questions related to their stress from the computer model and compare different questions in a graphical format. This is how the project has met the needs of the end user.

I'm aware that there are better ways this project could have been done and that if I was to do this again there are things I would change. For example, for my database for the computer model, I would have gathered more data and more diverse data as I'm aware that my data may be slightly biased. This would allow me to create a more accurate model as I would have more data and more diverse data.

For my Microbit, I would have tried to add more features. I am aware that meditation may not work for everyone, and it would be great if there were more options for the user. It could also be designed so that a user would get a random activity to help them reduce their stress (i.e. meditation, breathing exercises etc.). The program could determine how well each activity reduced that certain users stress and determine which activity to show them more using machine learning. These are a few ways in which I would improve my project.

It is also important to note that there are of course other factors that impact your stress levels other than the ones that I focused on. To get more of an accurate measurement you would a lot more data and parameters. For my project I had to make use of abstraction and heuristics to make this project work.

From completing this project, I can see how computer science has a clear social impact. Automation can be detrimental to society and cause problems such as loss of jobs. Through working on the project, I now understand the social impact of computer models, machine learning, AI and more. They have a lot of benefits to people but can also cause problems.
